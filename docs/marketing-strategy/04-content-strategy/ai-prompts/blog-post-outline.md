# AI Prompt: Blog Post Outline

> Use this prompt to generate a structured outline before writing a full blog post draft.

## Prompt Template

```text
You are a content strategist for Pike & West, an intimate art gallery and event venue in Germantown, Tennessee (Memphis area). Create a detailed blog post outline optimized for both readers and SEO.

## Content Details

**Working Title:** [Insert title]

**Primary Keyword:** [Insert primary keyword]

**Secondary Keywords:**
- [Keyword 1]
- [Keyword 2]
- [Keyword 3]

**Target Persona:** [birthday / anniversary / prom / corporate / graduation / baby-shower]

**Funnel Stage:** [awareness / consideration / decision]

**Search Intent:** [informational / commercial / transactional]

**Target Word Count:** [Insert target]

## Competitor Analysis

These are the top-ranking posts for this keyword:
- [Competitor 1 URL] - [brief notes on what they cover]
- [Competitor 2 URL] - [brief notes on what they cover]
- [Competitor 3 URL] - [brief notes on what they cover]

## Instructions

Create a detailed outline that:

1. **Title Options** - Provide 3 title variations that include the primary keyword
   - One question-based
   - One number-based (listicle style)
   - One statement-based

2. **Meta Description** - Write a 150-160 character meta description

3. **Introduction Approach** - Describe the hook and what the intro should accomplish (don't write it yet)

4. **H2 Sections** - Create 5-8 H2 sections that:
   - Flow logically
   - Include keywords where natural
   - Cover angles competitors missed
   - Build toward the CTA

5. **For Each H2 Section Include:**
   - The heading text
   - 3-5 bullet points of what to cover
   - Any specific examples or data points to include
   - Suggested word count for this section

6. **Content Enhancements** - Suggest:
   - Where to add lists, tables, or callout boxes
   - Internal linking opportunities
   - Image/visual opportunities
   - FAQ questions to potentially include

7. **Conclusion Approach** - Describe how to wrap up and transition to CTA

8. **CTA Recommendation** - Suggest the best call-to-action given the funnel stage

Output the outline in clean markdown format.
```

## Expected Output Format

The AI should return something like:

```markdown
# Blog Post Outline: Surprise Birthday Party Planning

## Title Options

1. **Question-based:** How Do You Plan a Surprise Birthday Party That Actually Stays Secret?
2. **Number-based:** 7 Steps to Planning a Surprise Birthday Party They'll Never See Coming
3. **Statement-based:** The Ultimate Guide to Surprise Birthday Party Planning for Adults

**Recommended:** Option 2 - combines keyword, number appeal, and intrigue

## Meta Description

"Learn how to plan a surprise birthday party that stays secret until the big reveal. Expert tips on coordination, venue selection, and keeping the guest of honor in the dark."

(158 characters)

## Introduction Approach

- Hook: Open with the anxiety of almost-blown surprise parties (relatable scenario)
- Establish expertise: "We've hosted dozens of successful surprise celebrations"
- Promise: "This guide covers everything from initial planning to the moment they walk through the door"
- Transition: Set up the step-by-step format

## H2 Sections

### 1. Decide If a Surprise Party Is Right for Them
**Word count:** 150 words

Cover:
- Some people genuinely hate surprises
- Questions to ask mutual friends/family
- Signs someone would love vs. hate a surprise
- Alternative: "surprise" elements within a known celebration

**Enhancement:** Add a quick yes/no checklist

---

### 2. Build Your Secret Planning Team
**Word count:** 200 words

Cover:
- Identify 2-3 trustworthy coordinators
- Assign specific roles (guest wrangler, decoy planner, etc.)
- Create a private group chat (name it something boring)
- The importance of a "single point of contact" for vendors

**Data point:** Mention that the #1 reason surprises get spoiled is too many people knowing details

---

### 3. Choose a Venue That Supports Secrecy
**Word count:** 200 words

Cover:
- Why private venues work better than restaurants
- Questions to ask venues about surprise party logistics
- Parking and arrival considerations
- Having a "decoy" destination

**Internal link opportunity:** Link to venue tour page

---

[Continue for remaining sections...]

## Content Enhancements

### Lists/Tables
- Checklist: "Is a surprise party right for them?" (Section 1)
- Timeline table: "Surprise Party Planning Timeline" (new section or within Section 4)
- Checklist: "Day-of Coordination Checklist" (Section 6)

### Internal Linking
- [Birthday Party Budget Guide] - when discussing costs
- [Memphis Catering Options] - when discussing food
- [Venue comparison guide] - when discussing venue selection

### Image Opportunities
- Featured: Surprised guest at party (stock or Pike & West photo)
- In-content: Example of a decoy invitation vs. real invitation
- In-content: Venue setup before guests arrive

### FAQ Section (Optional)
Consider adding at end:
- "How far in advance should you plan a surprise party?"
- "What if someone accidentally tells the guest of honor?"
- "How do you get someone to a surprise party?"

## Conclusion Approach

- Acknowledge the stress but emphasize the reward
- Quick recap of key coordination points
- Pivot to the relief of having a professional venue handle logistics
- Natural transition to CTA

## CTA Recommendation

**Primary CTA:** Schedule a Tour

**Approach:** "Planning a surprise celebration? Pike & West specializes in keeping secrets. Our team coordinates arrivals, manages timing, and ensures the big reveal goes perfectly. Schedule a tour to see how we make surprise parties stress-free."
```

## Outline Review Checklist

Before moving to draft stage:

- [ ] Title includes primary keyword
- [ ] Meta description is 150-160 characters
- [ ] 5-8 H2 sections with logical flow
- [ ] Each section has clear content direction
- [ ] Word count distribution makes sense
- [ ] Covers angles competitors missed
- [ ] Internal linking opportunities identified
- [ ] Enhancement opportunities noted (lists, tables, callouts)
- [ ] CTA matches funnel stage
- [ ] Overall structure serves reader intent

## Refinement Prompts

### Expand a Section

```text
Expand the outline for this section with more specific sub-points and examples:

[Paste section]

Include:
- 2-3 additional bullet points with specific details
- A real-world example or scenario
- A potential "Pro Tip" callout topic
- Any statistics that would strengthen this section
```

### Add Competitor Differentiation

```text
These are the H2 sections from top-ranking competitors for this keyword:

Competitor 1:
- [Their H2s]

Competitor 2:
- [Their H2s]

Competitor 3:
- [Their H2s]

My current outline has these sections:
- [Your H2s]

Identify:
1. What unique angles does my outline cover that competitors miss?
2. What important topics do competitors cover that I'm missing?
3. Suggest 1-2 additional sections that would make my content definitively better
```

### Validate Search Intent Match

```text
Review this outline against the search intent for "[primary keyword]":

[Paste outline]

Evaluate:
1. Does the content structure match what searchers are looking for?
2. Are we answering the main question quickly enough?
3. Is the depth appropriate for this query?
4. Should we adjust the funnel stage assumption?
5. Recommend any structural changes to better serve the searcher
```
