# Responding to Reviews

Guidelines and templates for responding to customer reviews across all platforms.

## Overview

- **Response Target**: Within 24-48 hours of review posting
- **Platforms**: Google, Facebook, The Knot, WeddingWire
- **Goal**: Build trust, show engagement, improve SEO

## Why Response Matters

### Trust Building

- 89% of consumers read business responses to reviews
- Responses show you care about customer experience
- Future clients see how you handle feedback

### SEO Benefits

- Google rewards businesses that respond to reviews
- Responses with keywords improve local search visibility
- Active engagement signals relevance to search engines

### Relationship Building

- Positive responses encourage repeat business
- Thoughtful negative responses can recover relationships
- Responses humanize your brand

## Response Time Guidelines

| Review Type          | Target Response Time |
|----------------------|----------------------|
| Negative (1-2 stars) | Within 24 hours      |
| Neutral (3 stars)    | Within 24-48 hours   |
| Positive (4-5 stars) | Within 48 hours      |

Faster is always better, but thoughtful responses beat quick generic ones.

## Responding to Positive Reviews

### Template Structure

1. **Thank them by name** (if available)
2. **Acknowledge something specific** they mentioned
3. **Reference the event type** to add context
4. **End with a warm close**

### Example Responses

**5-Star Wedding Review**

> Thank you so much, Sarah! We loved being part of your wedding celebration. The way you transformed the gallery space with those stunning floral arrangements was absolutely breathtaking. Wishing you and Michael a lifetime of happiness!

**5-Star Corporate Event Review**

> Thank you, James! It was a pleasure hosting your company's annual awards ceremony. We're thrilled the space worked perfectly for your presentation setup and that your team enjoyed the evening. We'd love to welcome you back for future events.

**5-Star Private Party Review**

> What a joy to be part of your milestone birthday celebration, Linda! Your guests clearly had a wonderful time, and we loved seeing the courtyard come alive with your decorations. Thank you for choosing Pike & West!

### Positive Response Tips

- Be genuine, not generic
- Mention specific details they referenced
- Keep it concise (2-4 sentences)
- Avoid promotional language
- Don't copy/paste identical responses

## Responding to Negative Reviews

### Template Structure

1. **Acknowledge and apologize** (without defensiveness)
2. **Thank them for the feedback**
3. **Offer to resolve offline**
4. **Provide contact information**

### Example Responses

**Service Complaint**

> Thank you for sharing your feedback, Michelle. We're sorry to hear your experience didn't meet the high standards we strive for. We'd very much like to learn more about what happened and make things right. Please reach out to us directly at <events@pikeandwest.com> so we can discuss this further.

**Communication Issues**

> David, thank you for taking the time to share this. We apologize for the communication challenges you experienced during your planning process. This isn't the experience we want for our clients, and we'd like to understand what went wrong. Please contact us at 901-206-5575 so we can address your concerns personally.

**General Dissatisfaction**

> We appreciate you sharing your experience, Jennifer. We're sorry Pike & West didn't meet your expectations. Your feedback helps us improve, and we'd welcome the opportunity to discuss your concerns directly. Please email us at <events@pikeandwest.com> at your convenience.

### Negative Response Tips

- Respond calmly, even if the review feels unfair
- Never argue publicly
- Take the conversation offline quickly
- Don't make excuses
- Focus on resolution, not explanation

## What NOT to Do

### Never

- Argue with the reviewer
- Get defensive or make excuses
- Reveal private information about the event
- Copy/paste identical responses to multiple reviews
- Ignore negative reviews
- Offer compensation publicly (do this privately)
- Use sarcasm or passive-aggressive language
- Blame vendors, staff, or circumstances

### Avoid

- Generic responses like "Thanks for your review!"
- Excessive promotional language
- Responding when emotional
- Lengthy explanations
- Asking them to change their review

## Platform-Specific Notes

### Google Reviews

- Most important for SEO
- Keep responses under 4,096 characters
- Use relevant keywords naturally (venue, weddings, Germantown)

### Facebook Reviews

- Can use slightly more casual tone
- Emojis acceptable but use sparingly
- Can tag the reviewer in response

### The Knot / WeddingWire

- Wedding-focused audience expects detailed responses
- Reference wedding-specific details
- Very important for wedding venue reputation

## Response Checklist

Before posting any response:

- [ ] Reviewed the original feedback carefully
- [ ] Response is personalized (not generic)
- [ ] Tone matches the situation (warm for positive, empathetic for negative)
- [ ] No defensive language
- [ ] Contact information included (for negative reviews)
- [ ] Grammar and spelling checked
- [ ] Response is appropriate length (2-5 sentences)

## Escalation Process

For reviews that require special handling:

1. **Potentially false/spam reviews**: Flag for removal on platform
2. **Legal concerns**: Consult with attorney before responding
3. **Ongoing disputes**: Limit public response, handle privately
4. **Media mentions**: Coordinate response with any PR strategy
