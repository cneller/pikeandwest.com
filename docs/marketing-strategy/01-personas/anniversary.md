## 3. Goals & Motivations

### Primary Goal

Host a sophisticated anniversary celebration that honors the marriage, thanks those who've supported it, and creates a memorable experience for the couple and guests.

### Emotional Drivers

- Deep love and appreciation for spouse
- Pride in marital longevity (especially significant milestones: 25th, 40th, 50th)
- Desire to model lasting love for children and grandchildren
- Gratitude for blessings and wanting to share joy
- Creating a legacy moment for family history

### Success Criteria

- Spouse feels honored and surprised (if applicable)
- Guests commenting on the elegance of the event
- Emotional moments captured in photos and video
- Smooth flow from ceremony to reception
- Staying within generous but defined budget
- Coverage in church/community communications

### Secondary Motivations

- Reconnecting with extended family and old friends
- Renewing wedding vows in meaningful setting
- Creating professional photos for home display
- Setting precedent for how family celebrates milestones

## 4. Pain Points & Challenges

### External Challenges

- Coordinating schedules of adult children (some may travel from out of town)
- Finding a venue that isn't a church fellowship hall or hotel ballroom
- Managing a guest list that spans generations
- Catering to diverse dietary needs
- Keeping the event a surprise (if planned as such)
- Parking and accessibility for elderly guests

### Internal Challenges

- Wanting perfection for such a significant milestone
- Budget guilt ("Should we spend this much on ourselves?")
- Stress of managing family dynamics during planning
- Fear the event won't live up to the significance of the moment
- Emotional weight of the milestone itself

### Objections (Why She Might NOT Book)

- "Is an art gallery appropriate for our style? We're traditional."
- "Can we have a religious ceremony element here?"
- "Is it big enough for our guest list?"
- "What if the art makes it feel cold or museum-like?"
- "Our church has a fellowship hall - is a venue really necessary?"

### Past Bad Experiences

- Country club event that felt impersonal
- Church fellowship hall that required too much DIY
- Hotel ballroom with generic decor
- Restaurant private room that was too cramped
- Event where food was disappointing despite high cost

## 8. Decision-Making Process

### Who Else Is Involved

- **Spouse** - May or may not know if it's a surprise
- **Adult children** - Often heavily involved, may be funding partially
- **Best friend/sister** - Trusted advisor
- **Event planner** - If using professional help
- **Church/pastor** - If ceremony element is included

### Timeline from Search to Booking

| Phase             | Duration  | Activities                               |
|-------------------|-----------|------------------------------------------|
| Research          | 2-3 weeks | Gathering ideas, initial searches        |
| Shortlist         | 1-2 weeks | Narrow to 3-4 venues                     |
| Tour              | 2-3 weeks | Visit venues, often with daughter/friend |
| Family discussion | 1-2 weeks | Share options, get input                 |
| Decision          | 1 week    | Compare options, finalize                |
| Booking           | 1-2 days  | Contract and deposit                     |

### Key Decision Criteria

1. **Elegance** - Does it feel special enough for this milestone?
2. **Capacity** - Can it accommodate our guest list comfortably?
3. **Versatility** - Can we have ceremony and reception?
4. **Accessibility** - Easy for elderly guests?
5. **Service** - Are staff responsive and accommodating?
6. **Reviews** - What do others say about their experience?

### Dealbreakers

- Space feels too casual or trendy
- Can't accommodate ceremony element
- Poor accessibility for elderly guests
- Staff seems inexperienced with formal events
- No examples of similar anniversary celebrations
- Restrictive rules that limit personalization
