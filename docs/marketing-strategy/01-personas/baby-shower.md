> "My sister is having her first baby and I want to throw her the shower she's been pinning on Pinterest for years. No church fellowship hall, no restaurant back room - something special."

## 2. Psychographics

### Values

- Family bonds and sisterhood
- Celebrating life's joyful moments
- Instagram-worthy aesthetics
- Creating memorable experiences
- Supporting the mom-to-be
- Attention to detail and design

### Lifestyle Indicators

- Very active on Instagram and Pinterest
- Follows party planning accounts and influencers
- Attends brunch with girlfriends regularly
- Shops at boutiques, Target, Amazon
- Enjoys hosting and entertaining
- Keeps up with trends in decor and events

### Personality Traits

- Enthusiastic and excited about the role
- Creative and design-minded
- Organized (or trying to be)
- Social and connected
- Generous with time and money for loved ones
- Possibly stressed about getting it "perfect"

### Social Influences

- The mom-to-be (must love it)
- Pinterest and Instagram inspiration
- Other baby showers she's attended
- Friends who've hosted similar events
- What she sees on social media from influencers

### Cultural Considerations

- Baby showers in the African American community are often large, co-ed events (not just for women)
- "Sprinkles" (smaller second baby showers) are becoming more common
- Gender reveals and baby showers sometimes combined
- Multiple hosts (co-aunties, friends, mothers) often share planning duties
- Professional photography is increasingly common for baby showers
- Elaborate decor themes expected (not just pink/blue basics)

## 3. Goals & Motivations

### Primary Goal

Throw a beautiful, memorable baby shower that makes the mom-to-be feel celebrated and loved while impressing the guests with thoughtful details.

### Emotional Drivers

- Love for her sister/friend and excitement about the baby
- Pride in being chosen to host
- Desire to create the "Pinterest-perfect" event
- Joy in the upcoming family expansion
- Competitive drive to throw a better shower than others she's seen

### Success Criteria

- Mom-to-be feeling overwhelmed with love (happy tears!)
- Guests commenting on how beautiful the venue/decor is
- Plenty of amazing photos for social media
- Smooth event flow from arrival to gift opening
- Staying on budget (usually shared with co-hosts)
- Mom-to-be getting everything she needs from registry

### Secondary Motivations

- Strengthening bond with sister/friend
- Showing off her event-planning skills
- Creating shareable social media content
- Building anticipation for the baby's arrival
- Establishing herself as the "cool aunt"

## 4. Pain Points & Challenges

### External Challenges

- Coordinating with co-hosts (often 2-4 people involved)
- Managing guest list with mom-to-be's input
- Finding a venue that isn't someone's house or a basic option
- Timing around mom's due date (not too early, not too late)
- Budget constraints (especially if paying alone)
- Coordinating decor, food, games, and gifts

### Internal Challenges

- Pressure to make it "perfect" (Pinterest expectations)
- Fear of disappointing the mom-to-be
- Stress of planning on top of regular life
- Uncertainty about what mom-to-be really wants
- Worry about forgetting important details
- Anxiety about things going wrong day-of

### Objections (Why She Might NOT Book)

- "Can we really afford a venue on top of decorations and food?"
- "Would it be easier to just do it at someone's house?"
- "Is an art gallery the right vibe for a baby shower?"
- "What if the mom-to-be thinks it's too fancy?"
- "Can we bring our own caterer/food?"

### Past Bad Experiences

- Baby shower at a home that was cramped
- Restaurant that was too loud for games and conversation
- Venue that didn't allow outside decor
- Event where the host was so stressed it showed
- Shower that felt generic and forgettable

## 8. Decision-Making Process

### Who Else Is Involved

- **Mom-to-be** - Must approve venue choice
- **Co-hosts** - Often 2-4 people sharing costs and decisions
- **Mother/mother-in-law** - Sometimes involved, sometimes just attending
- **Best friend(s)** - Input on decisions

### Timeline from Search to Booking

| Phase              | Duration  | Activities                                     |
|--------------------|-----------|------------------------------------------------|
| Research           | 1-2 weeks | Instagram/Pinterest browsing, initial searches |
| Shortlist          | 3-5 days  | Narrow to 2-3 realistic options                |
| Co-host discussion | 3-5 days  | Share options with other hosts                 |
| Mom-to-be approval | 2-3 days  | Confirm she loves the choice                   |
| Decision           | 1-2 days  | Finalize and book                              |
| Booking            | Same day  | Pay deposit, confirm date                      |

### Key Decision Criteria

1. **Aesthetic** - Is it Instagram-worthy?
2. **Price** - Can we afford it with our combined budget?
3. **Flexibility** - Can we decorate and bring food?
4. **Size** - Does it fit our guest list?
5. **Availability** - Is our preferred date open?
6. **Vibe** - Does it feel right for a baby shower?

### Dealbreakers

- Date not available
- No outside food/decorations allowed
- Too expensive for the budget
- Doesn't look good in photos
- Mom-to-be doesn't like it
- Slow or unhelpful communication
