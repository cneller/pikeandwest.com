## 2. Psychographics

### Values

- Education and achievement
- Family support and togetherness
- Honoring hard work and dedication
- Creating milestone memories
- Faith and gratitude
- Showing pride publicly

### Lifestyle Indicators

- Active in her church or faith community
- Involved in booster clubs, alumni associations
- Posts proudly about children's achievements on Facebook
- Attends every graduation-related event (senior night, awards, etc.)
- Maintains close relationships with extended family
- Plans family gatherings regularly

### Personality Traits

- Deeply proud and emotional about children's success
- Generous host who wants everyone comfortable
- Detail-oriented when it matters
- Community-focused
- Sentimental and tradition-minded
- Sometimes overwhelmed by all the graduation logistics

### Social Influences

- Other parents of graduates
- Her own parents/extended family expectations
- Church community
- What she sees other families doing

### Cultural Considerations

- In African American families, graduation celebrations are often large, multi-generational events
- First-generation college graduates receive especially significant celebrations
- Extended family travels from out of town for graduations
- Church community often invited to celebrations
- Food is abundant and high-quality
- Professional photography is common
- May combine celebration with religious thanksgiving element

## 3. Goals & Motivations

### Primary Goal

Host a graduation celebration that honors the graduate's achievement, brings together everyone who supported them, and creates lasting memories of this milestone.

### Emotional Drivers

- Overwhelming pride in graduate's accomplishment
- Desire to publicly honor the journey
- Gratitude for everyone who helped (teachers, coaches, family)
- Joy in reaching this milestone together
- For first-gen graduates: breaking barriers, making history
- Validation of parental sacrifice and support

### Success Criteria

- Graduate feeling truly celebrated and special
- Extended family and supporters in attendance
- Beautiful photos of the celebration
- Food that impresses and satisfies
- Smooth event without major stress
- Staying within budget while still being generous

### Secondary Motivations

- Showing appreciation to supporters
- Creating photo memories for graduate's future
- Networking opportunities for graduate
- Celebrating family achievement, not just individual

## 4. Pain Points & Challenges

### External Challenges

- Graduation season competition (May-June, everyone celebrating)
- Coordinating with graduation day timing (ceremonies often run long)
- Out-of-town family travel schedules
- Competing with multiple graduation events same weekend
- Budget stretched by gifts, graduation fees, celebration costs
- Weather uncertainty for outdoor options

### Internal Challenges

- Emotional overwhelm (proud but stressed)
- Guilt about cost vs. desire to celebrate big
- Pressure to match what other families are doing
- Stress of hosting large family groups
- Fear of the graduate not feeling special enough
- Juggling work and planning responsibilities

### Objections (Why She Might NOT Book)

- "Is a venue necessary? We could do this at home."
- "With graduation gifts to give, can we afford a venue?"
- "Will it feel too formal for a casual celebration?"
- "What if the graduation ceremony runs late?"
- "Can I bring food from my caterer/family members?"

### Past Bad Experiences

- Backyard party that was exhausting to host
- Restaurant that couldn't handle the group size
- Venue that felt impersonal or generic
- Event where food ran out
- Celebration that felt rushed due to venue time limits

## 8. Decision-Making Process

### Who Else Is Involved

- **The graduate** - Must approve, especially for college/grad school celebrations
- **Spouse** - Budget discussions, logistics
- **Her mother/mother-in-law** - Often weighs in on family events
- **Siblings** - May be co-hosting or contributing

### Timeline from Search to Booking

| Phase             | Duration  | Activities                        |
|-------------------|-----------|-----------------------------------|
| Research          | 1-2 weeks | Initial searches, gathering ideas |
| Shortlist         | 1 week    | Narrow to 2-4 options             |
| Tour/visit        | 1-2 weeks | See venues, ask questions         |
| Family discussion | 3-7 days  | Discuss with spouse, graduate     |
| Decision          | 2-3 days  | Compare options, make choice      |
| Booking           | 1-2 days  | Deposit and confirmation          |

### Key Decision Criteria

1. **Appropriateness** - Does it feel right for a celebration?
2. **Capacity** - Can it hold our family comfortably?
3. **Flexibility** - Food, timing, decor options
4. **Cost** - Within budget given other graduation expenses
5. **Atmosphere** - Warm, welcoming, not stuffy
6. **Convenience** - Location, parking, accessibility

### Dealbreakers

- Can't accommodate food flexibility
- Too formal or cold feeling
- Not available for needed date
- Significantly over budget
- Poor reviews about service
- Strict time limits without flexibility
