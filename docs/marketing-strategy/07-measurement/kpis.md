# Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

> The metrics that matter for Pike & West's marketing success.

## Primary KPIs (Business Outcomes)

These are the ultimate measures of marketing success. Everything else should ladder up to these.

| KPI                     | Target   | Frequency | Source     |
|-------------------------|----------|-----------|------------|
| Revenue                 | +50% YoY | Monthly   | Accounting |
| Bookings                | X/month  | Monthly   | HoneyBook  |
| Tour requests           | X/month  | Weekly    | HoneyBook  |
| Inquiry-to-booking rate | X%       | Monthly   | HoneyBook  |

### Notes on Primary KPIs

- **Revenue target** should be set during annual planning based on capacity and growth goals
- **Bookings target** varies by season - higher in engagement season (Oct-Feb), lower in wedding season (Apr-Oct)
- **Tour requests** are the critical leading indicator - track weekly to catch trends early
- **Inquiry-to-booking rate** measures sales effectiveness and lead quality together

## Secondary KPIs (Marketing Metrics)

These leading indicators predict future business outcomes.

| KPI                    | Target      | Frequency | Source |
|------------------------|-------------|-----------|--------|
| Website sessions       | X/month     | Weekly    | GA4    |
| Organic search traffic | X% of total | Monthly   | GA4    |
| Blog pageviews         | X/month     | Weekly    | GA4    |
| Time on page (blog)    | >2 min avg  | Monthly   | GA4    |

### Notes on Secondary KPIs

- **Website sessions** baseline should be established in first month of tracking
- **Organic search traffic** goal is typically 40-60% of total traffic for mature sites
- **Blog pageviews** measures content marketing effectiveness
- **Time on page** indicates content quality and engagement depth

## Setting Targets

### For New Metrics

1. Track baseline for 2-3 months before setting targets
2. Research industry benchmarks for context
3. Set realistic targets based on capacity and resources
4. Adjust quarterly based on performance

### Target Setting Formula

- **Stretch goal:** Baseline + 30%
- **Target:** Baseline + 15%
- **Minimum:** Maintain baseline

### Seasonal Adjustments

Wedding industry has strong seasonality. Adjust targets:

- **Peak engagement season (Oct-Feb):** +20% on inquiry/tour targets
- **Peak wedding season (Apr-Oct):** -20% on inquiry targets, focus on execution
- **Slow months (Jan, Jul):** Use for content creation and strategy work
